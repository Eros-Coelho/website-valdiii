The Story That Begins with the Father

Vlad III had a rather difficult childhood. While it does not excuse his actions, it certainly explains them, and there are historians in the 21st century who believe Dracula was merely a politician, not a monster or madman.

Vlad III was the second legitimate son of the then ruler of Wallachia, Vlad II Dracul, who was himself the illegitimate son of Mircea I of Wallachia. Vlad II was caught between two great rival nations, the Ottoman Empire to the south and east, and Hungary to the north and west. Despite his reputation as a fierce Christian crusader, the Hungarian warlord John Hunyadi did not extend his aid to Vlad, preferring the replacement of the voivode with a more pliable man over fighting the Turks.

In this precarious situation, Vlad II saw no alternative other than pledging his loyalty to the Ottoman Sultan, Murad II.

The Deal that Sealed Dracula's Life

During the years Vlad II Dracul supposedly served the Sultan, Murad II offered protection against the Hungarians, though the Voivode was clever, and prevented Ottoman control over Wallachia as much as he could.

Eventually, however, the Sultan realised something was amiss, and summoned Vlad II Dracul to him, along with his two elder sons, to pay homage and explain the situation. The Voivode suspected Murad had caught on, and instead took his two younger sons, Vlad III and Radu III.

In case Murad II decided to execute or imprison him and his sons, Vlad's elder son, Mircea, was in position to assume rulership of Wallachia. His worst fears came true when Murad accused him of conspiring against his allies, and threatened to kill him. As a last resort, Vlad II pledge his sons to the Ottomans, to be under the Sultan's care, and as colateral for the prince's actions. Basically, Vlad II offered his two sons as hostages to one of the most powerful leaders in the world, and whom he already planned to betray.

Children Hostage

Now formally given to the Ottoman Sultan, Vlad III and his brother Radu, also known as Radu the Handsome, were prisoners of the Ottomans. They were raised in Murad II's court, alongside with the Sultan's own son, Mehmed II, also known as Mehmed the Conqueror.

Vlad endured his captivity differently than his younger brother Radu, preferring to study warfare, philosophy, and train in sword fighting and riding, while Radu would convert to the Turkish way of life, and become an active member in the Sultan's court in the future.

Contrary to what their father thought, netiher Vlad nor Radu were butchered by the Ottomans, even when Vlad II Dracul supported a Christian Crusade against the Sultan. After the assassination of Vlad II Dracul and his eldest son Mircea, Vladislav II (son of Vlad II Dracul's cousin, Dan II) was placed ion the Wallachian throne by John Hunyadi, who by then had become Regent of Hungary, and fierce opposition to the House of Drăculești.
